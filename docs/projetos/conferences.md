## 2024

### Voice of the population and judge’s sensibility
**Conference of Empirical Legal Studies 2024**  
Atlanta, USA · November 2024

**Autores:** De Azevendo, Paulo Furquim & Wang, Henrique

Presented the results of the hypothesis of anchoring effect of judicial cases related to corruption. The paper observe two information shocks related to the
Carwash Operation, which unveiled a national scandal involving corruption among public
officials. Is observed statistically the anchoring effect of those events in judicial cases about corruption
but unrelated to the facts disclosed by the Carwash Operation.

### When in Rome… do less knowledgeable judges mimic their peers?
**Conference of Empirical Legal Studies Global Brazil 2024**  
São Paulo, Brazil · June 2024

**Autores:** De Azevendo, Paulo Furquim & Wang, Henrique

This paper examines how judges anchor their decisions on their peers’ judgments. It take advantage of the random distribution of judicial cases among the judges in each district and the semi-random migration of judges between districts. Is observed statistically the difference in behavior of the new judges compared to those that already are in that district in terms of decision text and the case outcome.
